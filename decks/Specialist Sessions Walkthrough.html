BUSINESS · SESSION 1 · CANVAS LITE · 105 MIN
Who pays, and can you defend the price?
01Fill a simple canvas: who pays, what for, how much, how often. The AI suggests a pricing pattern to start from.
02Check the three traps: is the payer really the user, can you defend the price against one real number, and what does this model always get wrong.
WHAT THEY LEAVE WITH
A one-line revenue statement (who pays, how much, how often, for what), with the price defended and the trap named.
HUDSON LAB VENTURES
04 / 22
BUSINESS · SESSION 2 · NAPKIN ECONOMICS · 135 MIN
Does one customer actually make money?
01Work out the money for one customer in one month. The AI gives quick, optimistic numbers.
02Put every cost back in, including getting the customer, which the AI leaves out. Then find the one number that, if wrong, breaks the model.
WHAT THEY LEAVE WITH
The unit economics, the break-even point, and the named risk. A sponsor reviews the math on Wednesday.

BUSINESS · SESSION 3 · THE 100-DAY PLAN · 105 MIN
What would the first 100 days look like?
01Business and market specialists come back together and turn the week's work into a plan, split into validate, build, and grow.

A sequenced 100-day plan with a budget and a day-100 scorecard.

08 / 22
MARKET · SESSION 1 · LANDSCAPE MAP · 105 MIN
How is the problem solved today, and where is the gap?
01List what already solves this, in three layers: direct competitors, indirect substitutes, and the do-it-yourself workarounds.
02Place them on a grid to see the empty space nobody fills, then name the riskiest thing you are assuming about it.
WHAT THEY LEAVE WITH
A map of the field and the single riskiest assumption to test.
HUDSON LAB VENTURES
09 / 22
MARKET · SESSION 2 · VALIDATION DESIGN · 135 MIN
How do you ask without leading the witness?
01Turn the riskiest assumption into questions that can come back no. Two rules: never mention your product, and ask what people actually did.
02Decide in advance what would count as a real signal, so a polite yes does not get mistaken for proof.
WHAT THEY LEAVE WITH
An interview plan they can run that same afternoon, out in the market.

MARKET · SESSION 3 · FIRST MOVES · PART OF WEDNESDAY
How do you reach the first hundred customers?

02Pick the first few and sequence the first hundred users over the early weeks.

AFTER EACH ROUND
Specialists rejoin and teach what they found.
01Each specialist gets five minutes to teach the rest of the team what they built, and where it is shaky.
02The team listens, then the contradictions between the three come out. The market read, the product scope, and the money do not always agree.
A team can only plan around what it understands. This teach-back is where the three specialties become one team again.

THE SAME IN EVERY SESSION
The student does the work. The AI structures it.
1 · THE STUDENT, FIRST
Works it out by hand: the numbers, the sketch, the plan.
2 · THEN THE AI
Structures it and fills it out, faster. But optimistic, and it leaves things out.
3 · THE STUDENT AGAIN
Checks it, puts back what is missing, decides what to trust.
The thinking is the student's, on both sides. The AI speeds up the middle; it does not replace the judgment. This happens live, in the room, with a facilitator watching.

ONE THING WE NOTICED
Each track works with the AI in its own way.
Market
does its own research first, then uses the AI to design sharper tests.
Product
sketches its own design first, then uses the AI to turn it into something to build.
Business
works out its own numbers first, then uses the AI to structure and pressure-test them.
All three do the thinking themselves and use the AI to speed up the middle. We did not design this as a rule; it is a pattern we saw once the sessions were built.
