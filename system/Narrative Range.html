The expressive set

Narrative range.

The archetypes give you the spine of a session. This page gives it pace and character — the slides that make a deck rise, fall, breathe and land. Each one is still only an arrangement of the same components, slots and diagram grammar; nothing here is invented by eye. They are grouped by the job they do in the room: how the story moves, and which kind of mind each reaches — the readers, the seers, the counters, the doers, and the ones who only lean in when a real person walks on.

VII

The narrative spine · rising & falling

A flat deck loses the room. These are the pacing tools: a quiet breath to reset attention, a hard turn that raises the stakes, a build that compounds, the peak it pays off in, and a cliffhanger that pulls them into the break. Tone and whitespace do the work — never decoration.

VIII

For the verbal mind · language & text

Some students think in sentences. They want the long version, the precise word, the argument laid out in order. Density is fine here — what matters is that text stays on the reading measure, on the grid, and never below the type floor.

41 · The reading pl-paper · reading measure

One column at the 816px reading cap, body type at 40px, with a single coloured assertion to mark the spine of the paragraph. A margin note gives the block air without crowding it.

THE LONG VERSION
Why search beats build.
A startup is not a smaller version of a big company. It is a temporary organisation built to search for a model that works. The search is the work. Anything you build before you have searched is a guess wearing a suit.

For the evidence‑driven mind · number & proof

Some students do not move until they see the figure. Give it to them at scale, in tabular numerals, with a source line — and never decorate a number it does not need. One headline, three measures, the spec, the comparison, the delta.

XII

Voice & character · the human hand

The slides that make the room lean in are the ones with a person on them. A character to follow, a real student's words, a note in the speaker's own hand, a scene that sets the stakes before a single concept is named. Character is what carries a deck the rest of the system cannot.
